Javier Sevilla Salcedo

I'm Javier Sevilla Salcedo, and I am currently diving into the world of research and teaching at the Systems Engineering and Automation Department at Carlos III University of Madrid. My academic journey began with a B.Sc. in Industrial Electronics and Automation Engineering from the University of Jaén, which opened up a path to a master's in Robotics and Automation right back at Carlos III University. I'm currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering, Electronics, and Automation, which is pretty exciting. Along the way, I've focused on theories and put what I learned into practice. I've had the fantastic opportunity to work in the robotics and automation field within the automotive industry, getting a taste of how these concepts are applied in the real world. I've also devoted over four years to some fascinating research areas—automating processes, refining robotics, exploring how robots can interact socially, and diving into Natural Language Processing. Part of my hands-on experience was designing a robotic guitar tuner for my bachelor's project. For my master's thesis, I developed NLP applications that help make social robots more engaging and intuitive. My current research interests are pretty broad, with a strong focus on Natural Language Processing. But I'm also passionate about Social Robotics, Deep Learning, and Deep Reinforcement Learning, as well as how robots cognitively perceive and interact with the world.

GERT: Transformers for Co-speech Gesture Prediction in Social Robots

GERT (Gesture-Enhanced Robotics Transformer), a set of three transformer-based models designed to predict gestures that align with a robot's verbal communication. The research evaluates and compares the effectiveness and efficiency of the three model variations, demonstrating that they all perform well (with F-scores ranging from 0.78 to 0.86) while highlighting the balance between model size and performance.
